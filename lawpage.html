Immoral Traffic (Prevention) Act, 1956

This act helps prevent trafficking and protects women from criminal exploitation, with special provisions for rehabilitation and rescue.

Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961

Criminalises demanding or giving dowry and supports women who face abuse related to marriage and in-law pressure.

Domestic Violence Act, 2005

Offers legal protection to women against physical, emotional, sexual, and economic abuse in domestic settings.

Commission of Sati (Prevention) Act, 1987

Prohibits the practice of sati and protects women from forced self-immolation through strict punishment and rescue measures.

Prohibition of Child Marriage Act, 2006

Protects young girls from early marriage by making child marriage illegal and offering legal remedies for victims.

How these laws empower women

The acts above offer formal protection, help survivors access support, and encourage awareness of rights. They are the foundation of a safer environment, stronger justice, and more equitable treatment for women.

  1. Immoral Traffic (Prevention) Act stops trafficking and provides support services for survivors.
  2. Dowry Prohibition Act prevents dowry-related abuse and protects married women from financial exploitation.
  3. Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act gives legal remedies and safe shelters for domestic abuse victims.
  4. Commission of Sati (Prevention) Act bans forced sati and punishes those who promote or allow it.
  5. Prohibition of Child Marriage Act safeguards girl children by preventing child marriage and underage unions.
  6. Sexual Harassment of Women at Workplace Act ensures a safe work environment with complaint mechanisms and penalties.
